Anti-Harassment Policy

We are dedicated to providing a harassment-free event experience for everyone, regardless of gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, or religion. We do not tolerate harassment of event participants in any form. Sexual language and imagery is not appropriate during the event. Event participants violating these rules may be sanctioned or expelled from the event at the discretion of the event organizers.

Harassment includes offensive verbal comments related to gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, religion, sexual images in public spaces, deliberate intimidation, stalking, following, harassing photography or recording, inappropriate physical contact, and unwelcome sexual attention. Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately.

If a participant engages in harassing behavior, the event organizers may take any action they deem appropriate, including warning the offender or expulsion from the event.

If you are being harassed, notice that someone else is being harassed, or have any other concerns, please contact organizers immediately.

Event staff will be happy to help participants contact hotel/venue security or local law enforcement, provide escorts, or otherwise assist those experiencing harassment to feel safe for the duration of the event. We value your attendance.

Know your limit

Alcoholism symptoms can vary from one person to the next, but there are some common signs that should never be ignored. One of the signs is a compulsion to consume alcohol or feeling like you need a drink badly. You may not be able to control how much or how often you drink alcohol, and you will need to consume increasing amounts of this substance in order to obtain the same effects. Another common symptom of alcohol addiction is drinking when you are alone or keeping this activity secret. All of these are indications that alcohol addiction treatment is needed and that an addiction is developing. Ignoring the problem will not make it go away, it will only get worse over time.

Event participants may engage in the consumption of alcohol during the event. Do not feel obligated or pressured to consume alcohol. If another participant pressures, taunts or otherwise engages in harrasing behavour please contact organizers immediately.
